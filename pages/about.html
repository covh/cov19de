covid19 Germany districts data visualization v02

Summary:

Study per district ("Kreis", "Landkreis"), the number of reported infections, in two ways:
  1. in comparison to other regions, via a variation of the conditional formatting table of Tomas Pueyo in his Coronavirus: Learning How to Dance --> chart 3
  2. as a time series plot of observables over time (cumulative total cases, daily new cases, smoothed with a simple moving average)

All is based on the excellent crowdsourced data by RiskLayer.com, a research group in Karlsruhe.

Assumption:

(At least until 2nd waves are happening ...) a good proxy for how dramatic the situation still is in a certain region, is what we call the "center day" of the daily new cases. The longer ago that center day, the more likely the outbreak is under control now.  The center day is calculated like this:

centerday = sum_over_all_daynumbers ( daynumber * daily_cases(daynumber) ) / total_cases

with
total_cases = sum_over_all_daynumbers ( daily_cases(daynumber) )
daynumber = 0 is the first day for which we have data (and incrementing for each later day), and
daily(daynumber) = the number of additional cases per daynumber (note that for the very first day (the day with daynumber = 0) that is undefined.)

in other words, the "center day" is: the average day, weighted with the number of new cases for each day.
